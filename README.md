# Dano_Airlines_customer_satisfaction_Analysis
This project analyzes passenger satisfaction data for Dano Airlines to identify the key factors influencing customer experience and provide recommendations for improving satisfaction levels. The analysis revealed an overall customer satisfaction rate of  43.45%, indicating that more than half of passengers  were either neutral or dissatisfied with their travel experience.

KEY FINDINGS
1.	Business class passengers recorded the highest satisfaction levels Economy and Economy plus passengers.
2.	Returning customers were more satisfied than first time customers, suggesting that customer loyalty is associated with better travel experience.
3.	Flight delays were strongly associated with lower customer satisfaction levels.
4.	Economy class passengers represented the largest group of dissatisfied customers.

   
RECOMMENDATIONS
1.	Reduce flights delays through improved scheduling and operational efficiency.
2.	Improve  Economy class service offerings to enhance passenger comfort.
3.	Develop retention programs targeting first time customers.
4.	Continue maintaining high service standards in Business class while extending similar improvement to other travel classes.

CONCLUSION

Improving operational efficiency and enhancing customer service quality can significantly increase passenger satisfaction and strengthen Dano Airline’s competitive position within the airline industry.
